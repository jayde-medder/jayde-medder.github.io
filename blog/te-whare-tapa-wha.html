Te Whare Tapa Whā

Te Whare Tapa Whā is a model of the 4 dimensions of wellbeing developed by Tā Mason Durie in 1984 to provide a Māori perspective on health. When having difficulty with one wall/aspect of our lives we can work on strengthening the other walls until we can rebuild it.

My Wellbeing Plan

Wall

Activities

Taha Tinana (Physical Wellbeing)
  • Going for a walk two times a week.
  • Gardening on the weekends.
  • Occasional sound work on the weekends.
  • Getting good amounts of rest.
  • Eating Well
Taha Whānau (Family Wellbeing)
  • Dinner with my family once a week. Or more!
Taha Hinengaro (Emotional and Mental Wellbeing)
  • Listening to audiobooks before bed.
  • Talking with friends and family every day.
  • Knitting
  • Cooking good food and eating well.
Taha Wairua (Spiritual Wellbeing)
  • Playing music with friends regularly
  • Sound work
Whenua (Interconnections to the land and environment)
  • Walks around Port Chalmers and Aramoana
  • Gardening
