A description of your strengths and limitations when it comes to learning

What do you think your biggest strengths and limitations will be in Bootcamp?

I have learned a few strategies to deal with my limitations. I started using a chrome extension for dictation when online reading becomes difficult. I realised also that exploring different approaches to a problem, and when/why they’re used is important in understanding features of my code. For example I might use some array method, but also look into other ones and figure out which is most suitable and why. This is something I often do in the refactoring stage of a problem or as I am implementing it. I’m also better at taking breaks, and set myself times for them.

Another limitation I have noticed is my tendency to resist asking for help. I think once I can get over this I will feel a lot more comfortable with where I’m at. I think it’s important to keep in touch with facilitators and team members to get more of a sense of how I am doing. I’ve found that when I speak to peers about their work I am intrigued by the differences in their approaches and realise that being inspired in this way is important for my growth.

What skills (non-technical core/human skills) would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

Sprint 2 Answer: “Teamwork and in particular communicating regularly. I work well independently but need to remember to check in with my team/facilitators often.”

I seem to be getting a little bit better at communicating with team members but could talk more with facilitators. I enjoy talking with team members so I hope to do this more regularly and do a bit more peer programming.

What do you think your biggest non-technical challenge at Bootcamp will be?
